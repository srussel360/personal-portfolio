Girls Who Code

I have been a part of Girls Who Code since September 2018, first as a general member and later as the social chair. As a chair, I aid in running the University of Michigan branch of Girls Who Code, the college-level organization for Girls Who code aimed at supporting and increasing the number of women in tech fields. We accomplish this through hosting coding workshops and social events for general member and community outreach programs for high school aged students in the area.


Global Scholars Program

I have been a part of the Global Scholars Program since August 2018, first as a intern and now as a facilitator. As a collaborative group facilitator, I lead a group of underclassmen to help them complete an internship with an international organization. I facilitate their development of professional skills as well as a better understanding of global social justice, helping them learn how to incorporate invaluable lessons of intercultural interaction into their everyday lives.


University Unions League Board of Governors

I have been a part of the Global Scholars Program since September 2018, first as a general board member and now as the chair. As the chair, I lead a group of students, staff, and alumni to discuss and reach consensus about decisions regarding a historical university union, including renovations, policy change, and scholarship distribution. My day to day tasks include collaborating to create meeting agendas, arranging presenters for monthly meetings, and keeping meeting discussions on track.
